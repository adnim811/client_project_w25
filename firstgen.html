First-Generation Students at U-M

Who is a First-Generation Student?

At the University of Michigan, undergraduates and graduate students are considered first-generation college students if neither parent or guardian has completed a four-year college or university degree. This definition includes students with other family members (siblings, cousins, grandparents, etc.) who have completed a four-year degree.

First-Generation Support Resources

First-Generation Gateway

The First-Generation Student Gateway serves as a starting point to get connected to resources for first-generation students. Located on the third floor of the Student Activities Building in the Office of Academic Multicultural Initiatives, the Gateway offers:

  • Information on resources for first-generation students
  • Connections to partner offices that support first-generation students
  • Meetings with the First-Generation Project Manager
  • Study and meeting spaces

For more information, visit the First-Generation Gateway.

Financial Support

Various financial aid options are available to assist first-generation students, including:

  • Kessler Scholarship: A scholarship specifically for incoming first-generation college students to cover tuition. Students must apply for this scholarship. For more details, visit the Paying for School page.
  • Emergency Funds: Student emergency funds are available for anyone on campus, with additional funds from individual schools and colleges. More information can be found on the Student Emergency Fund page.

Programs and Services

The University of Michigan offers a variety of programs and services to support first-generation students, such as:

  • Comprehensive Studies Program (CSP): Provides academic support through courses, holistic advising, and co-curricular programming.
  • Multi-Ethnic Student Affairs (MESA): Offers support and programming through celebrations, educational offerings, and building social capital through the lens of race and ethnicity.
  • Office of New Student Programs (ONSP): Engages students through orientation, mentorship, and welcome programs.

For a comprehensive list of programs, visit the Programs page.
